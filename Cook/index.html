Ingredients

Main Dough

  • 250 g Flour, e.g., braided bread flour
  • ½ - ¾ Tsp Salt
  • 1 Tsp Sugar
  • 38 g Butter, softened
  • 10.5 g Yeast, crumbled
  • 1.5 dl Milk, lukewarm

Decoration

  • ½ Hazelnuts, walnuts, sultanas, pearl sugar, Beaten egg, for brushing

Preparation

Prepare

40min

Cook / Bake

25min

Let rise

1h

On the table in

2h 5min

Dough

Mix flour, salt, and sugar, forming a well. Place butter in the well. Dissolve yeast in milk and pour it into the well. Knead into a smooth dough. Allow it to rise at room temperature, covered, until doubled in size. Do not knead the dough further.

Decorating the Grittibänz

Set aside a small amount of dough for decoration. Divide the remaining dough into 6 parts (adjust based on your desired quantity). Roll each piece into an oval shape, approximately 12 cm in length (the length of a baked Grittibänz).

Cutting

On both sides, use a dough scraper to cut out a triangle for arms and legs. Make additional cuts in the dough for arms and legs.

Decorations

Press raisins, nuts, etc., deeply into the dough. Use the reserved dough to create decorations such as scarves, hats, belts, etc. Attach decorations with a little water. For hair, use scissors to cut spikes into the head.

Baking

Preheat the oven to 180°C (convection) or 200°C (top/bottom heat). Place the Grittibänze on a prepared baking sheet, brush with beaten egg, and bake in the lower half of the preheated oven for approximately 20 minutes. To check for doneness, tap the underside of the Grittibänze – they are ready when it sounds hollow.
